Founded by Chicago Public Schools students in 2016, the Student Policy Council is a Chicago-based, student-led initiative dedicated to promoting researching progressive education policy while publishing viable policy proposals.

Board of Advisors

William Ayers, Ed.D.

William Ayers, formerly Distinguished Professor of Education and Senior University Scholar at the University of Illinois at Chicago (UIC) has written extensively about social justice and democracy, education and the cultural contexts of schooling, and teaching as an essentially intellectual, ethical, and political enterprise. His books include A Kind and Just Parent; Teaching Toward Freedom; Fugitive Days: A Memoir; Public Enemy: Confessions of an American Dissident; On the Side of the Child; Teaching the Personal and the Political; To Teach: The Journey in Comics; Teaching Toward Democracy; and Race Course: Against White Supremacy. His latest book is Demand the Impossible!: A Radical Manifesto.

Pauline Lipman, Ph.D.

Dr. Pauline Lipman is a professor of Educational Policy Studies and Director of the Collaborative for Equity and Justice in Education at the University of Illinois at Chicago. She is a co-director of the Data and Democracy Project and works with community organizations to address educational injustices and transform urban schools. She is the author of numerous books including The New Political Economy of Urban Education: Neoliberalism, Race, and the Right to the City; High Stakes Education and Race: Inequality, Globalization, and Urban School Reform; Class and Power in School Restructuring.

Byron Sigcho, Ph.D.

Dr. Byron Sigcho earned his Ph.D. in Policy Studies in Urban Education and has served as a researcher for the Collaborative for Equity and Justice. An Ecuadorian immigrant, teacher, and activist, Byron has been a strong advocate for quality neighborhood schools. Byron started a Spanish Literacy program for Head Start parents at the University of Illinois at Chicago where he is currently a Visiting Researcher. He is also the Vice President of the Hispanic Literacy Council of Chicago, a former Local School Council representative, a core member of Teachers for Social Justice, and the Executive Director of the Pilsen Alliance where he fights for economic, environmental, and educational justice, spearheading grassroots community initiatives.
